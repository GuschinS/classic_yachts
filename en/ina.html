s/y Ina

‘Ina’ is a yacht type J-80 commonly known as ‘Jotka’. It is steel yawl rigged heavy cruiser, 45 feet long, designed in 1967 by Henryk Kujawa and Józef Szymańda.

Ina’s line was modified in relation to the original. It was created in the ‘Płock series’ when design changes were made by the Interster office, under the leadership of Juliusz Strawiński. Above all, the aft part was redesigned, which was widened to increase buoyancy, and the afterdeck was raised.

Ina's hull was made by the Płock River Shipyard in 1981. It was created as the number 2 out of total 10 ordered by the Polish Yachting Association at the end of the 1970s. Then, along with three alike sisters ‘Rega’, ‘Dziwna’ and ‘Piana’, it was transferred to the buildings of the Szczecin Yacht Shipyard. Launching took place in 1987. After which all four ‘Jotkas’ began to serve as training yachts at the PZŻ Sailing Training Centre in Trzebież.

After 30 years, the ‘Jotkas’ in Trzebież stood on the shore. The well-worn ‘Ina’ was bought back in 2018 and then refurbished and retrofitted. We replaced fragments of the bottom, side and deck plating, removed all layers of paint and put a new set of coatings. ‘Ina’ got new electronics, an electrical switchboard, sails, a life raft, and dozens of trinkets. Further things are waiting for installation, including new diesel heating.
